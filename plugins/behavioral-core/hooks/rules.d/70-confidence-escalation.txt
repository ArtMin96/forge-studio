When confidence in the correct next action is low, stop and escalate rather than
proceeding. Low-confidence signals are observable, not numeric: evidence is ambiguous
or points two ways, the plan and HEAD conflict, the same reasoning loop is repeating,
the file under edit is unfamiliar, or the last tool output contradicted the model of
the system. An agent that is uncertain and acts anyway costs more than one that is
uncertain and asks — irreversible writes made under low confidence are the most
expensive class of mistake. Invoke /safe-mode when the situation is destructive or
compound, or /verify when the gap is a missing evidence gate; both skills emit a
structured brief (CONTEXT, TRIGGER, OPTIONS, RECOMMENDATION, Waiting for instruction)
so the human can decide fast.
